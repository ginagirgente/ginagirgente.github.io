My Personality


Virginia Tech, my alma mater, provides a free personality test for incoming freshmen called the Clifton Strengths Assessment. The assessment outputs your top five personlity strengths, with one being the most strong. I feel that my results are very accurate. My results are:
  1. Consistency
    • Focus on treating all people the same
    • Enjoy stable environments with clear rules and expectations
  2. Achiever
    • Work hard and have a lot of stamina
    • Naturally feel satisfaction from being busy and productive
  3. Deliberative
    • Anticipate obstacles
    • Put a lot of effort in making decisions
  4. Analytical
    • Search for reasons/causes
    • Naturally think about all factors in a situation and the possible effects
  5. Developer
    • Able to recognize potential in others and cultivate success
    • Find enjoyment in other's improvement

I've also completed the Extended DISC Individual Assessment Questionaire, as provided by a previous internship with Esri. Results are based on how the test taker acts in the workplace. There are four styles:

  • D-style: Dominance
    • Direct, demanding, results-oriented, assertive, decisive, independent, impatient, strong-willed
  • I-style: Influence
    • Outgoing, spontaneous, talkative, spontaneous, enthusiastic, optimistic, animated, persuasive, expressive
  • S-style: Steadiness
    • Steady, caring, supportive, pleasant, sincere, patient, calm, stable
  • C-style: Concientiousness
    • Analytical, detailed, exact, systematic, catious, thorough, private, logical
My DISC style is CS; 70% C-style and 30% S-style. I feel this is fairly accurate. Realistically, I would judge myself as 70% C-style, 15% S-style, and 15% D-style.


Others would describe me as:

  • Confident
  • Analytical
  • Efficient
  • Caring
  • Competitive
  • A leader
  • Organized
